Inspired by Mary Poppendieck's talk (which I shared in my earlier blog post) I started looking for ways to learn more about Microservices.

I found that for many new subjects one of the best approaches to "brake yourself in" is actually watching a good presentation. For the topic of Microservices I have chosen four of such presentations, and after listening to them my mind does not go completely blank when the term "Microservices" is mentioned.

GOTO 2014 • Microservices • Martin Fowler

This is Martin Fowler's talk from the year 2014. It is relatively shorter (~ 26 mins) than the others I saw (40+ mins), but provides an excellent introduction into the topic. If I had to choose just one talk to watch on the subject - I would choose this one.

GOTO 2015 • Microservices @ Spotify • Kevin Goldsmith

This presentation explains how microservices work in big internet company, but also shares the details on the intricacies of software development process in Spotify.

GOTO 2016 • The Idiot’s Guide to Quashing Microservices • Hani Suleiman

This talk concentrates on more practical aspects of the challenges with which teams are faced before and after moving to Microservices architecture, for example:

  • security;
  • logging and monitoring;
  • integration testing;
  • availability and performance, etc.
The author suggests the strategies and specific tools for many of the mentioned chalenges.

GOTO 2016 • Pragmatic Microservices • Randy Shoup

Randy Shoup talks about why sucessful technology startups did not start with Microservices-basesd applications and why planning for Microservices from the start might not be a good idea. Also he gives an advice on how to approach moving from a single monolith architecture to number of independent services.

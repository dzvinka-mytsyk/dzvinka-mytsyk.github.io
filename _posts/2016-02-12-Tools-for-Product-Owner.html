Depending on the specifics of the development projects, Product Owners on the daily basis perform various activities. To make their days more effective they (of course!) use many different tools. And in this blog post I wanted to share the list of mine.

The list is organized by the category (type of activity) and at the end there is also my "sort of wish-list" of the products I'd like to try out.

Creating / Sharing Product Backlog

The heart and soul of Product Owner's world - Product Backlog. Whenever possible, I do still prefer having it in some sort of version controlled table (MS Excel/LibreOffice Calc), but I see benefits of other solutions nonetheless.

  • LibreOffice Calc (www.libreoffice.org/discover/calc/)
  • MS Excel (products.office.com/en-us/excel/)
  • Atlassian Confluence / Jira (www.atlassian.com)
  • Post-its / Index Cards - sometimes there is nothing better :)

Process Management / Tasks Tracking

As Product Owner, you still need to organize yourself, manage and track your own tasks as well as keep up with colleagues' progress.

  • Trello (trello.com) - simple Lean tool to visualize workflows
  • Atlassian Jira (www.atlassian.com/software/jira) - probably the most powerful instrument for issues tracking

Product Roadmap and Canvas

For Roadmap and Canvas I prefer the lightest approach of them all - simple templates. Even if then they "don't integrate with everything".

  • Product Canvas template (www.romanpichler.com/tools/product-canvas/) - template developed by Product Management expert Roman Pichler
  • Product Roadmap template (www.romanpichler.com/tools/product-roadmap/) - template developed by Product Management expert Roman Pichler

Wireframing and sketching

I use the following tools for documenting UI visions, sketches and layouts:

  • Balsamiq (www.balsamiq.com)
  • Canva (www.canva.com/)

Knowledge Management

Notes, various drafts, meeting minutes as well as internal and processes documentation:

  • Atlassian Confluence (www.atlassian.com/software/confluence)

UML / BPMN / Flow Diagrams

For clarifying algorithms, documenting business processes and creaing all sorts of diagrams and charts:

  • Gliffy (www.gliffy.com) - online and simple
  • Draw.io (www.draw.io) - love the google.docs integration
  • MS Visio (products.office.com/en-us/visio/flowchart-software) - a powerful instrument available when I have to work on Windows

Databases

Working with databases is a great skill for anyone involved in software development project. Insights you can get by simple queries, quick reports you can whip up not waiting for anyone, test cases you can run are just a few of the long list of examples.

  • PostgreSQL (www.postgresql.org) - is described as the most advanced open source database, and I agree
  • SQL Power Architect (www.sqlpower.ca/page/architect) - good for data analysis and ER-diagrams
  • pgAdmin (www.pgadmin.org) - GUI client for PostgreSQL
  • MySQL (www.mysql.com/products/community/) - some web-based projects simply require it, so we have to comply ;-)
  • Oracle/Toad/Sybase/MS SQL Server - all these I used to work with on enterprise level projects, but not in the recent years. I have great memories though :-)

Coding and Version Control

Occasional scripting and compiling is good for PO not to loose the grip on technical side of things: it helps to understand and appreciate all the complexities development teams are facing.

  • Git (git-scm.com) - every more or less important work product should be version controlled, that's it
  • GitHub (github.com) - for sharing and collaboration
  • Atom (atom.io) - ever since I stumbled upon this one, no other editor existed for me for writing my sql scripts as well as editing xml, json, html or css.

Things to try

New software products become available every day, so it is important to look for new useful things from time to time, e.g. on resources like www.producthunt.com and www.capterra.com.

Here is my wish-list for nearest future:

  • Waffle.io (waffle.io) - Project Management solution powered by GitHub Issues and Pull Requests
  • Kissmetrics (kissmetrics.com) - Customer Analytics Platform
  • Craft (craft.io) - a tool for planing User Stories, Sprints and Roadmaps
  • Planio (plan.io) - Project Management instrument with Issue Tracking, Project Management, Version Control, Help Desk, Wikis and Chat
  • Proto.io (proto.io) - a tool for creating high-fidelity fully-interactive prototypes
  • tinyPM (www.tinypm.com) - Agile Product Management tool
